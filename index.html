We are computer science and computer information students at the University of Dayton working with Riverside Research to create an AI solution to detect circular reporting in technical documents

Sprint 1:

We met with our clients and started developing our project. We made a basic frontend, backend, and database with the ability for the user to enter text and store it in the database. We began setting up the backend for a locally running LLM and can prompt the LLM and receive a response.

Sprint 2:

We improved the UI, making a sleek and modern design. Our project now has user authentication, the ability to upload files (PDF and DOCX) with the text extracted in the database, and the user can now see the history of prompts they submitted.
